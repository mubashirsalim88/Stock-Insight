Introduction to Stock Markets

Why Should You Invest?

  • Fight Inflation: Investment helps counter the rising cost of living over time, commonly referred to as inflation.
  • Create Wealth: Investing allows individuals to build a corpus over time, achieving financial goals.
  • Meet Financial Aspirations: Investments help fulfill life's financial goals and dreams.

Types of Investments

  • Fixed Income Instruments: Includes fixed deposits (FDs) and bonds that provide a guaranteed return over a fixed time period.
  • Equity: Involves buying shares of publicly listed companies through stock exchanges.
  • Real Estate: Investment in property, including apartments, land, and commercial buildings, generating rental income and capital appreciation.
  • Commodities: Investment in precious metals like gold and silver.

Who Participates in the Stock Market?

Various participants play critical roles in the stock market:

  • Retail Investors: Individuals investing in the stock market for personal accounts.
  • Institutional Investors: Large organizations or entities that hold significant stakes in companies.
  • Mutual Funds: Funds that invest in companies meeting specific criteria on behalf of investors.
  • Investment Banks: Assist companies in raising capital through public offerings.

Stock Market Timings in India

  • Pre-Opening Session (9:00 AM - 9:15 AM): Allows investors to place and modify orders before the market officially opens.
  • Normal Trading Session (9:15 AM - 3:30 PM): The primary session where most trading occurs.
  • Post-Closing Session (3:40 PM - 4:00 PM): Investors can place orders at the closing price.

Other Financial Markets

In addition to the stock market, other financial markets include:

  • Forex Market: The world's largest financial market, where currencies are exchanged.
  • Cryptocurrency Market: A decentralized digital currency market, where cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin are traded.
  • Commodities Market: Trading in physical goods like gold, silver, oil, and agricultural products.

Types of Traders

Traders in the market use various strategies based on their risk appetite and time horizons:

  • Intraday Traders: Buy and sell within the same day to profit from short-term price movements.
  • Swing Traders: Hold stocks for days or weeks, targeting short- to medium-term trends.
  • Position Traders: Hold stocks for months or even years, focusing on long-term growth.
